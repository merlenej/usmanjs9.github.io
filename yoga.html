GENERAL RELATABLE THOUGHTS EVERYONE HAS DURING HOT YOGA

As a Los Angeles native, I was basically raised on green juices, spirituality, and masking torture as “self-care.” One of the most popular ways to make yourself cry in the name of wellness is with hot yoga, or Bikram yoga if you want to be specific. If you’ve never been, it’s basically 60 to 90 minutes of bending your body in a series of poses in a room with the temperature ranges from 80 to 110°F. Because someone was like, “Let’s put regular yoga into an oven and watch everyone melt into a puddle of sweat!”


But we all continue to go. Why? Because the benefits of hot yoga include burning calories and increasing your flexibility, and we also know that by the end, we’ll feel good, glowing, and filled with endorphins. And sweaty. We’ll definitely be sweaty.


Tips for Surviving Hot Yoga

If you want to make your hot yoga experience a little less uncomfortable, remember to bring a ton of water, towel or two – one to wipe off sweat and one to place on your mat to prevent slipping –, a change of clothes, a yoga mat, an igloo, a therapist, and a large pizza. (Wait. That last one is for after class.) Wear as little or as light clothing as possible, and avoid wearing makeup, because it will drip off your face, I don’t care how sweatproof it says it is. To provide some solidarity in the pain and suffering of this sweat-inducing workout, here are a few of the many thoughts that I have before and during a hot yoga class.
